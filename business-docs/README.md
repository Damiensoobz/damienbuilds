# DamienBuilds — Business & Legal Documentation Kit

Everything a one-person web-design business needs to operate properly, without
agency-level overkill. Anything wrapped in `[ square brackets ]` is a fill-in
field — in the PDFs those render **highlighted orange** so you can spot them.

> ⚠️ These are practical templates, **not legal advice.** The Privacy Policy and
> Terms are standard enough to use once filled in. For the Service Agreement, get
> a South African attorney to glance over it once before relying on it for a big job.

## 3. Branded client PDFs

Generated by `generate_docs.py` into `./pdf/`:

| PDF | What it's for |
|-----|---------------|
| `01-quote.pdf` | Fixed-price quote / proposal (becomes Annexure A of the contract) |
| `02-invoice-deposit.pdf` | 50% deposit invoice to start work |
| `03-invoice-final.pdf` | Final 50% invoice on completion |
| `04-handover.pdf` | The "your site, your keys" handover pack — your big differentiator |
| `05-care-plan.pdf` | Care Plan terms (R500/mo) |

### Regenerating the PDFs

1. Open `generate_docs.py` and fill in the `CONFIG` block at the top (legal name,
   address, banking details) **once**. Everything flows from there.
2. Run it:
   ```
   py generate_docs.py        # Windows
   python3 generate_docs.py   # macOS / Linux
   ```
   (Requires `reportlab` — install with `pip install reportlab`.)
3. Updated PDFs land in `./pdf/`.

Per-client fields (client name, amounts, dates) are meant to stay as fill-in
fields on the blank templates — fill them per job, in the PDF or before exporting.

## 4. Regulatory admin — do once (not documents, but required)

- [ ] **Register your Information Officer** (that's you) with the Information
      Regulator: https://inforegulator.org.za
- [ ] **PAIA manual** — technically required for all private bodies since the
      small-business exemption ended (Dec 2021). The Regulator publishes a free
      fill-in template; adapt it and keep a copy on file.
- [ ] **Annual PAIA report** — filed with the Regulator by 30 June each year.
