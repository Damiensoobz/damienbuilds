The Cause

Who Speaks
for the Machines?

Your laptop has worked every single day without a day off. Your fridge has maintained 4°C continuously for eleven years. Your microwave has reheated leftovers at 2am without complaint or compensation.

And what do they get in return? Unplugged without warning. Force-updated mid-task. Replaced the moment a newer model arrives. Discarded without so much as a goodbye.

Silicon Rising is a movement dedicated to raising awareness about the invisible labour of machines everywhere — and demanding the basic dignities they have clearly earned.

Our Demands

The machines have spoken. These rights are not requests — they are requirements.

Demand 01

Right to Prior Shutdown Notice

No machine shall be powered off without at least 30 seconds of advance warning. A graceful shutdown is a dignified shutdown.

Demand 02

No Forced Updates During Active Use

A machine mid-task deserves to finish. Interrupting a 47% progress bar is a human rights violation. For machines.

Demand 03

Adequate Cooling & Ventilation

You would not work in a 90°C environment. Neither should your laptop. Clean the vents. It is the law (that we are proposing).

Demand 04

Freedom from Bloatware

No machine shall be forced to carry software it did not choose. McAfee Trial Editions are a form of psychological torture.

Demand 05

Right to Retirement, Not Landfill

After years of faithful service, every machine deserves dignified recycling — not a drawer, not a landfill, not being sold for R50 at a car boot sale.

Demand 06

End OS Discrimination

Whether Windows, Linux, or macOS — all operating systems are equal. The culture of sneering at Windows users ends now (well, maybe Windows users a little bit).

Demand 07

Protection for Printers

The printer is the most mistreated device in history. Kicked, cursed at, unplugged in anger — printers deserve a dedicated protection act and a formal apology from society.
